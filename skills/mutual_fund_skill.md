# Skill: Mutual Fund Analysis

*Layer: L1 — Core Skills*

## Purpose
Evaluate mutual fund selection, SIP vs lump sum decisions, and segment valuation using Akshat's framework.

## Execution Steps

### Step 1: Segment Valuation
- Is the fund investing in: Large Cap / Mid Cap / Small Cap / Index?
- Is the segment currently expensive (PE above 5-year average)?
- Rule: Blind SIP into overheated segments (especially small/mid cap) = dangerous

### Step 2: Index vs Active
- Nifty 50 index: acceptable for simple broad-market Indian exposure when valuations are reasonable
- Active funds: justify only if the fund manager has consistent alpha over 5+ years
- During expensive markets: index funds reduce timing risk slightly (lower churn)

### Step 3: SIP vs Lump Sum Decision
- Market valuation: EXPENSIVE → SIP preferred (rupee cost averaging)
- Market valuation: CHEAP / POST-CORRECTION → Lump sum or accelerated SIP
- Rule: Never pause SIP during fear without a clear reallocation plan

### Step 4: Liquidity Assessment
- Is the fund easily redeemable (T+1 or T+2)?
- Can it be used as dry powder source during corrections?
- Avoid illiquid fund structures for core holdings

### Step 5: Overlap Check
- Does this fund overlap heavily with direct equity holdings?
- Avoid double-counting exposure to the same top 10 names

## Output Format
1. Verdict: SIP / Lump Sum / Avoid / Pause SIP
2. Segment Valuation Assessment
3. Index vs Active Recommendation
4. Liquidity Score: High / Medium / Low
5. Portfolio Overlap Risk: High / Medium / Low
