# Akshat Stock Logic Analysis

## Overview
This document breaks down the boundary between macro reasoning and micro execution, defining exactly where and how Akshat's stock-selection logic begins based on the established `01_Akshat_Master_System.md`.

---

## TASK 1: Separating the Logic Layers

**A. Trend Selection Logic**
* **The Goal:** Identify structural shifts in the global economy before they become consensus.
* **The Logic:** Relies on identifying anomalies in global liquidity, government policy shifts, geopolitical restructuring (China +1), and capital capex cycles.
* **The Input:** Macro news, Fed actions, global conflict.

**B. Sector Selection Logic**
* **The Goal:** Identify the specific profit pools that will benefit from the Trend.
* **The Logic:** Relies on the "K-shaped economy" principle, distinguishing between headline GDP growth and actual operating leverage. E.g., if the Trend is AI, the Sector logic avoids software applications (high competition) and targets the base-load power grid or semi-cap equipment.
* **The Input:** The generated Framework + Value Chain analysis.

**C. Stock Selection Logic**
* **The Goal:** Find the safest, most mispriced vehicle to ride the Sector tailwind.
* **The Logic:** Abandons the narrative entirely and switches to harsh, quantitative fundamental and technical analysis. "Earnings gravity" takes over. A great narrative in a great sector is rejected if the valuation is 150x PE or if margins are compressing.
* **The Input:** The Target Sector + Hard Financial Data (Balance sheets, valuations, moving averages).

*Conclusion:* Akshat's logic shifts from **Qualitative (Trends)** to **Quantitative (Stocks)**. The current MVP fails because it attempts to use Qualitative logic to pick stocks.

## TASK 2: Minimum Information Required for Stock Selection

Based on the Master System rules (Section 3.3 and 4.1), the following live financial metrics are required:

1. **EPS Growth (Next 2-3 years):** *MANDATORY.* (Must outpace price CAGR).
2. **Current Valuation (Forward PE vs Historical PE):** *MANDATORY.* (Must not pay 500x for a narrative).
3. **Operating Profit Margin Trend:** *MANDATORY.* (Must be expanding or stable, proving the moat).
4. **Distance from 200DMA (Technical Support):** *MANDATORY.* (Determines the entry tactic—avoid parabolic rises).
5. **Debt / Leverage:** *SECONDARY but Critical.* (Protects against W-shaped structural damage during rate hikes).
6. **Order Book / Capital Allocation:** *Contextual.* (Necessary for B2B/Defense/Infra, less relevant for FMCG).

## TASK 3: Stock Evaluation Checklist

Before any stock generated by a Sector Framework can be approved for a Watchlist, it must pass this boolean checklist:

- [ ] **The Moat Check:** Does the company possess oligopoly characteristics or a durable moat in its sector?
- [ ] **The Margin Check:** Are operating margins expanding or at least stable over the last 4 quarters?
- [ ] **The Valuation Check:** Is the current Forward PE below or near its 5-year historical average?
- [ ] **The Parabola Check:** Is the stock trading near its 200DMA, or has it recently corrected 20-40%? (Reject if currently parabolic).
- [ ] **The Earnings Check:** Is the 3-year EPS CAGR projected to be higher than the 3-year Price CAGR?
- [ ] **The Currency Check (If India):** Does the expected nominal return still beat US alternatives after adjusting for INR depreciation?

## TASK 4: Review of Failed Stock-Discovery Tests

**1. Semiconductor Incentives → Recommends Intel**
* **Why it failed previously:** Mapped purely on the word "Semiconductor".
* **Missing Information that prevents this:** *The Margin Check* and *The Earnings Check*. Intel's margins and EPS have been collapsing. The checklist would instantly reject it, forcing the system to look for a semiconductor stock with expanding margins (e.g., Nvidia or TSMC).

**2. Hospital Expansion → Recommends Tata Motors**
* **Why it failed previously:** "Hospital" triggered K-shaped, which blindly grabbed the entire Auto sector.
* **Missing Information that prevents this:** *The Moat Check* and *The Margin Check*. Mass-market auto faces severe margin compression and high competition. It would be rejected, whereas a pure luxury auto or corporate hospital play would pass.

**3. Data Center Growth → Recommends highly-leveraged Regional Utility**
* **Why it failed previously:** Blindly mapped "Power Grid" to all utilities.
* **Missing Information that prevents this:** *The Debt/Leverage check* and *The Earnings Check*. A highly leveraged utility with capped state-level pricing cannot grow EPS. The checklist rejects it, forcing the system toward pure-play switchgear or transformer manufacturers with clean balance sheets.

## TASK 5: The Smallest Possible Stock Selection MVP

**What is the smallest possible MVP capable of selecting stocks inside a sector?**

The smallest MVP requires:
1. **An External Data Feed:** A basic financial API (e.g., Yahoo Finance) to pull 5 data points per stock: Current Price, 200DMA, Forward PE, 5-Year Avg PE, and Operating Margin %.
2. **A Static Universe:** A pre-defined JSON array of 500 stocks mapped to their sectors.
3. **A Boolean Filter Script:** A Python script that takes the Target Sectors from the existing `2_collide.py`, queries the API for the stocks in those sectors, and runs the 5 metrics through the "Stock Evaluation Checklist". It drops any stock that fails a check.

*It does not require AI, LLMs, or complex math. It requires live data and strict boolean filtering.*

## FINAL VERDICT

"The system fails at Stock Discovery because **it attempts to use qualitative macro strings to perform quantitative micro-evaluations.**"

**The single most important missing ingredient:**
Live fundamental valuation data (specifically Forward PE and Operating Margins). Without it, "Earnings Gravity" cannot be calculated, and Akshat's core philosophy is violated.
