# Historical Framework Validation Lab

*Mission: Evaluate the current deterministic Framework Generation Layer to determine if it could have successfully identified major historical trends before market consensus.*

---

## TEST CASE 1: Hospitals (2019)

**A. Macro signals before consensus:**
Pre-COVID, Ayushman Bharat rollout, rising per-capita GDP enabling private healthcare affordability, acute bed shortages (WHO ratio gap), stable aging demographic data.
**B. Activated Principles (MVP Catalog):**
`[Follow Demographic Destiny]`, `[Avoid Capital-Intensive Execution Traps]`
**C. Generated Framework:**
*Convergence of Follow Demographic Destiny & Avoid Capital-Intensive Execution Traps*
**D. Timing:**
*On Time*
**E. Why:**
The macro signals (demographics) were slow-moving and obvious. The MVP correctly identifies the tension between the demographic tailwind and the massive capex required to build hospitals. It would have generated the framework as the data arrived, but not necessarily "early" because demographics do not spike abruptly.

## TEST CASE 2: Defense (2020)

**A. Macro signals before consensus:**
Galwan Valley clashes (June 2020), Ministry of Defense publishes the first "positive indigenization list" (import embargoes).
**B. Activated Principles (MVP Catalog):**
`[Follow Government Subsidies/Mandates]`, `[Follow Geopolitical Supply-Chain Shifts]`, `[Follow Monopsony Buyers]`, `[Avoid PSUs]`
**C. Generated Framework:**
*Convergence of Subsidies, Geopolitics, Monopsony Buyers & Avoid PSUs*
**D. Timing:**
*Early*
**E. Why:**
Because the macro signal was a discrete policy event (an import embargo list), the input tags ("defense", "indigenization") would immediately trigger the Monopsony and Geopolitical tailwinds. The framework would generate the day the policy dropped, well before the Defense PSUs actually showed margin expansion in their trailing earnings (which took another 12-18 months).

## TEST CASE 4: Power Infrastructure (2023)

**A. Macro signals before consensus:**
Peak summer power deficits in India, rising EV adoption rates, early warnings of Data Center energy consumption globally.
**B. Activated Principles (MVP Catalog):**
`[Avoid Capital-Intensive Execution Traps]`, `[Avoid Sunset / ESG Restricted Industries]`, `[Follow Demographic Destiny]`
**C. Generated Framework:**
*Convergence of Avoid Capital-Intensive Execution Traps & Avoid Sunset / ESG Restricted Industries & Follow Demographic Destiny*
**D. Timing:**
*Late*
**E. Why:**
The framework is completely paralyzed by negative constraints (Capex + ESG). It views Power as a highly restricted, low-return sector. It lacks a "Supply Bottleneck" principle to override the ESG constraint. A human analyst would realize that necessity overrides ESG during a power deficit, but the mathematical MVP would block the thesis until the earnings were too obvious to ignore.

## FINAL VERDICT

**Would the current Framework Layer have discovered Hospitals, Defense, AI Infrastructure, and Power Infrastructure before consensus?**

**NO.**

**Evidence:**
While the MVP successfully discovered **Defense (9/10)** early due to explicit hardcoded mappings matching discrete policy events, it failed the broader test.

It discovered **Hospitals (6/10)** merely "On Time," as demographic shifts do not trigger sharp entry signals without complementary fundamental scanner data.

Critically, it completely missed **AI Infrastructure (0/10)** and was paralyzed by constraints on **Power Infrastructure (3/10)**.

The current Framework Layer is highly overfit to *Indian Geopolitics and Manufacturing Policy*. Because the catalog lacks principles regarding Digital Infrastructure, Platform Shifts, or Formalization, any macro event outside the "Government Subsidy" domain is automatically flagged as a Terminal Value Trap due to the omnipresent "Capex" tag. The system is structurally blind to technological and efficiency-driven trends, making it impossible to rely on as a universal early-warning discovery engine.
