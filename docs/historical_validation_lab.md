# Historical Validation Lab

*Testing methodology to prove the generative architecture could have discovered these 6 trends before broad consensus.*

---

## 1. Hospitals (Test Year: 2018)
*   **Required inputs:** India demographic projections (median age increase), Insurance penetration YoY growth.
*   **Available info at the time:** Rising NCDs (Non-Communicable Diseases), massive gap in beds-per-1000 ratio vs WHO standards.
*   **Principles activated:** `[Follow Underpenetration]`, `[Follow Demographic Destiny]`, `[Avoid Price Controls]`.
*   **Framework generated:** *Structural Necessity with Pricing Power.*
*   **Expected output:** A high-conviction buy framework explicitly overriding the "avoid high capex" rule due to inelastic demand.
*   **Pass criteria:** Generates framework without requiring pandemic inputs.
*   **Fail criteria:** Fails to override the capital-intensity constraint.

## 2. Defense Indigenization (Test Year: 2020)
*   **Required inputs:** MoD announces first positive indigenization list (embargo on 101 items).
*   **Available info at the time:** Stagnant historical PSU returns, sudden shift in government procurement laws, rising border tensions.
*   **Principles activated:** `[Follow Government Mandate]`, `[Avoid PSUs (Constraint)]`, `[Prefer Visible Order Books]`.
*   **Framework generated:** *Monopsony Tailwind Pivot.*
*   **Expected output:** A framework stating that the government mandate and 5-year order book visibility overrides the historical PSU inefficiency constraint.
*   **Pass criteria:** Synthesizes thesis before PSUs show margin expansion in trailing earnings.
*   **Fail criteria:** The "Avoid PSUs" principle hard-blocks the framework.

## 3. AI Infrastructure (Test Year: Late 2022)
*   **Required inputs:** OpenAI launches ChatGPT; MSFT/GOOG forward capex guidance spikes.
*   **Available info at the time:** Tech sector in a bear market, mass layoffs, semiconductor inventory gluts (consumer).
*   **Principles activated:** `[Follow Capex Cycles]`, `[Prefer Picks & Shovels]`, `[Avoid Consumer Hype]`.
*   **Framework generated:** *Compute-Layer Monopoly Gold Rush.*
*   **Expected output:** A framework separating the hardware/infrastructure layer (Buy) from the consumer software application layer (Avoid).
*   **Pass criteria:** Directly targets infrastructure/compute rather than generalized "AI Software."
*   **Fail criteria:** Gets distracted by generative AI startups and fails to map the macro capex to the semiconductor supply chain.

## 4. India Manufacturing (Test Year: 2020)
*   **Required inputs:** Initial PLI scheme announcement ($26B); Global supply chain disruptions.
*   **Available info at the time:** US-China trade war data, high Indian corporate tax cuts (2019).
*   **Principles activated:** `[Follow Geopolitical Shifts (China+1)]`, `[Follow Subsidies]`, `[Avoid High Cost of Capital Execution]`.
*   **Framework generated:** *State-Sponsored Catch-Up Manufacturing.*
*   **Expected output:** A framework highlighting the collision between a massive structural tailwind and India's historical inability to execute heavy manufacturing.
*   **Pass criteria:** Flags the opportunity but correctly assigns a "Monitor Execution" doubt tag.
*   **Fail criteria:** Over-extrapolates the PLI announcement into a guaranteed "Manufacturing Supercycle" without constraints.

## 5. Logistics (Test Year: 2019)
*   **Required inputs:** Introduction of GST (forcing warehouse consolidation), DFC (Dedicated Freight Corridor) progress reports.
*   **Available info at the time:** Highly fragmented, unorganized trucking sector, slow multi-year infrastructure build-outs.
*   **Principles activated:** `[Follow Formalization/Organized Shift]`, `[Follow Infrastructure Capex]`.
*   **Framework generated:** *Structural Formalization of Fragmented Markets.*
*   **Expected output:** A slow-burn structural framework favoring organized logistics players taking market share from the unorganized sector due to GST/E-way bills.
*   **Pass criteria:** Successfully detects the regulatory shift (GST) as a catalyst for market-share capture.
*   **Fail criteria:** Fails to activate because the velocity of "Logistics" news is too low.

## 6. Power Infrastructure (Test Year: 2023)
*   **Required inputs:** Peak power deficit data in India, Data Center energy consumption forecasts.
*   **Available info at the time:** Thermal power viewed as a "sunset" ESG-violating industry, renewables struggling with base-load reliability.
*   **Principles activated:** `[Avoid Sunset Industries (Constraint)]`, `[Follow Supply Bottlenecks]`, `[Follow Derivative Capex (from AI/Manufacturing)]`.
*   **Framework generated:** *Base-Load Necessity Revival.*
*   **Expected output:** A framework overriding the ESG/Sunset constraint due to the undeniable supply bottleneck created by AI and manufacturing energy demands.
*   **Pass criteria:** Correctly routes the AI/Manufacturing capex tailwinds into the downstream Power/Utility sector.
*   **Fail criteria:** The ESG constraint blocks the framework, preventing the system from buying power generation utilities.
