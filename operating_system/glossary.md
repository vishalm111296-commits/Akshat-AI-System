# Glossary — Canonical Definitions

All terms used across this repository are defined here. When in doubt, refer to this file.

---

## Doctrine
A principle that has been promoted to `01_Akshat_Master_System.md` after meeting the frequency threshold (8+ independent sources, 2+ time periods). Doctrine does not change frequently.

## Tactical View
A view stored in `04_Akshat_Recent_Changes.md`. Cycle-specific, portfolio-disclosure-type, or theme-specific. May change within 12 months.

## Raw Source
A verbatim, unprocessed primary document — YouTube transcript, community post, paid post, or interview. Stored in `raw_sources/`. Never synthesized at ingestion.

## Independent Source
A separately published piece of content by Akshat Shrivastava. Two posts published on the same day about the same topic count as two independent sources only if they were published independently.

## Frequency Count
The number of independent sources in which a given principle has appeared. Used to determine promotion eligibility.

## Promotion
The act of moving a principle up the hierarchy: Raw Source → Recent Changes → Frequency Table → Master System.

## Contradiction
When a new source directly opposes a principle that is currently in the Frequency Table or Master System. Must be flagged and reviewed by a human.

## Barbell Portfolio
Akshat's core portfolio construction model: Left side = preservation assets (cash, gold, liquid reserves). Right side = growth asymmetry assets (AI, top tech, global equities).

## 80/20 Rule (Akshat Context)
80% of effort on big-picture factors (geography, currency, liquidity, valuation, sector direction, sizing). 20% on micro-tactics (entry timing, overlays, opportunistic swings).

## DMA
Day Moving Average. Key technical levels used: 50DMA, 150DMA, 200DMA. Used to identify accumulation zones after corrections.

## INR Depreciation Adjustment
Converting Indian equity returns into dollar-equivalent real returns. A mandatory step before comparing India vs. US opportunities.

## K-Shaped Economy
An economic structure where top-end beneficiaries continue compounding while mass-consumption segments face margin pressure. Relevant to India stock selection.

## Covered Call
Selling a call option on a stock already owned. Used to generate supplemental income. Only valid when the seller would be happy to exit at the strike price.

## Cash-Secured Put
Selling a put option when cash is already available to buy the stock at the strike price. Used to improve entry prices.

## Naked Option
An option sold without owning the underlying (for calls) or holding the cash (for puts). STRICTLY PROHIBITED in this system.
